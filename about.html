About Bugema University

Bugema University (BU) is a private Christian university in Uganda that is affiliated with the Seventh-day Adventist Church. It is one of the institutions in the global Adventist education system and is known for combining academic education with spiritual and moral development.

📍 Location

Main campus: Bugema, Luweero District, Uganda

About 32–33 km north of Kampala on the Gayaza–Zirobwe Road.

The campus covers about 640 acres of land in a rural environment.

📅 History

  • Founded in 1948 as Bugema Missionary Training School.
  • Originally trained teachers and pastors for the Seventh-day Adventist Church in East Africa.
  • Later became Bugema Missionary College and then Bugema Adventist College.
  • In 1994, it received a government license to operate as a university.

🎓 Academic Programs

Bugema University offers certificate, diploma, undergraduate, and postgraduate programs in different fields. Some major schools include:

  • School of Science and Technology
  • School of Business
  • School of Education
  • School of Agriculture & Applied Sciences
  • School of Health & Allied Sciences
  • School of Social Sciences
  • School of Theology & Religious Studies

Examples of courses:

  • Software Engineering
  • Business Administration
  • Education
  • Public Health
  • Journalism
  • Agriculture

🏫 Campuses and Study Centres

Besides the main campus in Bugema, the university also has other centers such as:

  • Kampala Campus
  • Kasese Campus
  • Arua Centre
  • Mbale Centre

The Kampala Campus serves mainly working professionals and was accredited by the National Council for Higher Education (NCHE) in 2017.

🎯 Mission and Motto

Motto: "Excellence in Service."

Mission: To provide holistic Christian education that prepares students for productive service to society and to God.

Computing and Informatics

Computing is the study of the principles, design, and applications of computers and computational systems. It encompasses a broad range of topics, from theoretical algorithms to practical software development and systems architectures, equipping students to solve complex problems through code.

Informatics is the science of information processing, focusing on the intersection of people, data, and technology. It involves designing secure, efficient, and user-friendly systems to manage, store, and extract meaningful insights from vast amounts of data.
